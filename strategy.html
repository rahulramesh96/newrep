Eco-friendly materials

While we can't say for sure how much gasoline these super cars consume, we do know that some steps have been taken to make race cars more environmentally friendly. 

Fiber Reinforced Polymer (FRP) composites, such as carbon fiber, have long been utilized to construct lightweight race vehicles. 

NASCAR, on the other hand, may take things a step further by inventing an environmentally friendly flax fiber seat for their cars. 

Cost-efficient and sustainable, these Eco-friendly seats offer enough strength and stiffness to counteract vibrations for a more comfortable ride. 

The low density and high stiffness levels of flax fibers improve other aspects of the vehicleâ€™s performance as well including compression, damping, and torsion. The seat can do all of this while still allowing the car to hit race-winning speeds. 

Taking inspiration from the previous carbon fiber seats, the flax fiber composite seats were built up after reverse-engineering the old seat models 

The key difference is that the new flax fiber seat emits 75% less CO2 than the carbon fiber unit.
