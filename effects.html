EFFECTS

NASCAR emits more than 200000 tonnes of carbon which leads to environmental pollution. 

Racers consume around 6,000 gallons of race fuel for , compared to 730 gallons per year for the average driver. 

Race cars not only gulp gas, but they also eat tires. During the race, the majority of the squad uses 8-10 sets of tires. For every barrel of oil burnt to create tires, around 698 pounds of CO2 is released into the atmosphere. Just from the worn tires during a race, this adds up to around 149,090 pounds of CO2.

Until 2007, Each NASCAR event released more than 10 Kg of lead into the air of adjacent areas (about the same amount than an airport emits in a year), which persisted long after fans had departed the track. 


The majority of Americans feel that vehicle emissions are the most significant contributor to their carbon footprint. The television compound at each NASCAR racetrack is the largest in professional sports, and it is in operation 38 weeks out of the year. While this consumes a significant amount of electricity. According to data from the US Department of Energy, electricity is responsible for the majority of our environmental effect. 

Environmentalists says NASCAR emits tons of greenhouse gases and also filling the landfill with huge amount of garbage such as plastic packed food items/plastics bottles.
